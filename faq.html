Due to the high power requirements of these keyboards, the absolute cable length (including coil) should be kept as short as possible. For 550 cable (26/28AWG) it's recommended to cap the total cable run at ~270cm. For all other keyboards, keep the total cable run under 390cm.

To resolve power issues:

  • Check the BIOS to see if your motherboard has options to increase USB port voltage
  • Use the keyboard/cable with a back panel USB port or a port that plugs directly into the motherboard
  • Use the keyboard/cable with a powered USB hub
  • Ensure that there aren't too many USB devices on a single interface. Too many devices and they may end up fighting for power
  • Set "XHCI Pre-Boot Mode" to "Disabled" in the BIOS
  • Try different ports (USB 2.0 / USB 3.x)

    • ***Do not mix detachable cables across different vendors***

  • Cables with detachable connectors (aviator, GX16, YC8, etc) may not be compatible with detachable cables from other vendors. Using incompatible detachable sides together can cause damage.
  • USB C 5-pin connectors are for USB C to USB C cables only. Detachable USB C 5-pin cables are only compatible with other detachable USB C 5-pin cables.

    • Cable diameter varies depending on whether or not the cable has techflex.
    The diameter can also vary slightly depending on the paracord spool.
    550 cable diameter with techflex is ~5.2mm and w/out techflex is ~4.2mm.

    Jumbo cable diameter (22AWG) is ~7mm.

    Techflex adds a second color tone, diameter, coil strength, and overall feel to the cable.

    Coils made with thinner cable (e.g. without techflex) use more total cable because of the thin cable. Coils made with thicker cable (e.g. with techflex) use less cable. Using less total cable is an advantage from a power delivery perspective since cables lose power over distance (attenuation).

    Thicker cables also produce slightly taller coils. For example, a 5.2mm cable (with techflex) coiled on a large rod will be taller than a 4.2mm cable (without techflex) coiled on the same rod..
